## 1.0 Introduction

### 1.1 Background
In Spring 2025, our final project for Engineering 11 was to design and prototype a practical engineering product. After brainstorming and evaluating multiple ideas, we decided to create a door sensor. The purpose of this device is to detect the opening of a door using a magnetic switch and to alert the user with a sound via a mini piezo buzzer. This simple, compact, and effective system enhances home security and accessibility, especially for individuals needing sound-based alerts.

## 2.0 Design Problem Formulation

### 2.1 Filling a Niche
While many commercial door sensors exist, many are expensive, bulky, or require Wi-Fi and smartphone integration. Our goal was to build a low-cost, battery-powered, stand-alone door sensor with audible feedback, suitable for dorms, homes, or tool sheds.

### 2.3 Performance Requirements
- Cost under $200  
- Fit within **1.5 × 1 × 1 cm** housing  
- Sound a piezo buzzer when the door opens  
- Run on a small battery **~3–6 months**  
- Indoor use (32°F–104°F / 0°C–40°C); keep dry; avoid direct sun/moisture

### 2.4 Customer Satisfaction Curves
User satisfaction was mapped vs. runtime, price, and power consumption.

## 6.0 Parametric Design

### 6.1 Design for Assembly (DFA)
- Minimal part count (magnet, reed switch, buzzer, battery, housing)  
- Snap-fit/screw-in housing; standard, low-cost parts  
- Coin-cell holder (simple battery swap); minimal tools required

### 6.2 Design for Function (DFF)
- Durable plastic housing; **low power** (~6 months on CR2032)  
- **~85 dB** buzzer; safe **3V** operation; compact and reliable  
- Simple wiring, easy battery replacement, good magnetic alignment

## 7.0 Engineering Analysis

### 7.1 Power & Battery Life
- Piezo ≈ **15 mA** when active  
- ~20 door openings/day, **2 s** each  
- Idle leakage ≈ **0.05 mA**  
- Average ≈ **0.057 mA** → Estimated battery life **~5–6 months**

### 7.2 Response Time
- Reed switch closes **< 1 ms**  
- Audible alert latency **< 10 ms**

### 7.3 Sound Output
- Buzzer emits **~85 dB** at 1 m

### 7.4 Range & Alignment
- Reliable triggering within **~10 mm**; alignment is important  
- Prefer **neodymium** magnets for stronger fields

## 8.0 Testing

**Test 1 – Circuit Functionality:** Buzzer activates immediately when magnet moves away; resets when closed.  
**Test 2 – Alignment Sensitivity:** Consistent activation up to ~10 mm.  
**Test 3 – Sound Audibility:** ~85 dB at 1 m in a quiet room.  
**Test 4 – Battery Life Projection:** ~5–6 months (based on usage assumptions).

## 8.5 Conclusion & Recommendations
The system met goals for cost, reliability, and simplicity.  
**Future work:** add wireless alerts (BLE/LoRa), weatherproof the housing, switch to PCB, add LED/adjustable tones, and consider AA/rechargeable power options.

## Appendix

### Bill of Materials (BOM)
- Reed Switch — $1.60  
- Mini Piezo Buzzer — $3.00  
- CR2032 Battery — $1.00  
- Battery Holder — $0.60  
- SPDT Switch — $0.60  
- Neodymium Magnet — $0.80  
- Plastic Housing — $4.50  
- Jumper Wires/Solder — $0.40  
**Total:** ~$12.50

### Operation
1) Mount sensor & magnet housings on door/frame  
2) Insert CR2032 battery (correct polarity)  
3) Power ON — buzzer sounds when door opens  
4) Power OFF to disable
